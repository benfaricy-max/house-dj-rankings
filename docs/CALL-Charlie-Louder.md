# Call prep — Charlie, Louder UK

**Who:** Charlie — lead promoter / talent buyer, [Louder](https://louderuk.com/) (independent UK
electronic promoter; year-round club + live + outdoor shows e.g. On The Beach Brighton;
recently took [Live Nation investment](https://newsroom.livenation.com/news/new-investment-supports-independent-uk-promoter-louder/)).
**Format:** feedback call. **You requested it to learn, not to sell.**

> Charlie is close to an ideal design partner: a buyer who signs real fees, books across
> club/festival/outdoor, and — post-Live-Nation — has volume and a team. If the number is
> useful to *him*, it's useful to the market. Treat this call as research that happens to
> also be a pitch.

---

## The one goal

Leave the call with **permission to run a fair-value read on the next 3 acts Louder is
weighing**, and his honest answers to three questions. A "design partner forming" is the only
outcome that matters. Everything else (does he love the UI, does he sign up) is noise today.

**Do not** pitch £75/£300 hard. Collect his number instead (Q3).

---

1. Pick **one act Louder actually books or is weighing** (ask yourself: what's on their
   autumn/outdoor 2026 radar?). If you don't know one cold, use a strong-buy act in their lane
   from the index — all live reads, today:

   | Act | Read | Current band → demand-implied | Fits Louder |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | **Prospa** | strong-buy **+79%** | £18–40K → £35–70K | UK, WHP/DC-10, festival-ready |
   | **ANOTR** | strong-buy **+79%** | £18–40K → £35–70K | festival headliner, big outdoor draw |
   | **PAWSA** | strong-buy **+133%** | £8–18K → £18–40K | tech-house, club + outdoor |
   | **KETTAMA** | strong-buy **+133%** | £8–18K → £18–40K | UK/Irish, fabric, club |

2. Open that act's **Value Gap report** (`#/value/<slug>`), generate a **Pitch Link**
   (framed "Buying / offer case", 7-day expiry), and have it ready to screen-share or paste.
3. Be ready to say, honestly: **the fee bands are curated estimates, not transacted fees** —
   the *demand* side (RA, Beatport, 1001Tracklists, streaming, routing) is real. That candour is
   the pitch, not a weakness. (You hold 0 verified fee anchors today — this call is also how you
   start getting them. See "After".)

## Open (15 seconds — set it as feedback, not a sell)

> "I'm not going to pitch you. I built a neutral demand index for house and techno — the idea is
> a fair-fee read that *neither* the agent *nor* the buyer is spinning. You book for a living, so
> you're exactly who can tell me if it's actually useful or if I'm fooling myself. I've got a live
> read on [Act] to react to, then three questions, then I'm out of your hair."

---

## The demo (one act, let the number do the work)

Screen-share the [Act] Value Gap report. Say the number, then stop talking:

> "[Act] is reading **strong-buy** — current fee band [£X], demand-implied [£Y]. The reason:
> RA bookings and Beatport are running ahead of the fee — the rooms know before the rate-card
> moves. This is the whole product: one neutral number, with the method shown."

Then surface the supporting lenses if he leans in: **routing/saturation** (is the region already
saturated — does a date there feel less exclusive?), **club-vs-viral** (is the demand real
booking demand or TikTok noise?), and the **paste-ready negotiation line**.

---

## The three questions (the real point of the call)

**Q1 — does the number change a real decision?** *(validates the product)*
> "When you look at [Act] for a date — does this read change how you'd make the offer, or is it
> telling you something you already knew?"
>
> Listen for: a reaction to the £ gap. "Huh, I'd have offered more/less" = the wedge works.
> "Already knew that" = the value isn't the number — dig for what is.

**Q2 — who sets what Louder pays?** *(economic buyer + process, esp. post-Live-Nation)*
> "When you land on a headliner fee — is that your call, or does it go past someone now?"
>
> Listen for: where the sign-off sits, and whether Live Nation changed it. That tells you if
> Charlie is the buyer, the champion, or both — and whether the real account is Louder-the-team
> (Team tier) not Charlie-the-individual.

**Q3 — what would you pay, and per what?** *(pricing reality + the unit)*
> "If this saved you from overpaying on one booking a season — what's that worth? And would you
> think about it per booking, per month, or per seat for the buying team?"
>
> Listen for the **unit he answers in** — that's your value metric. Don't defend a price; write
> down his number.

**Close (never end open):**
> "Last thing — can I run this on the next three acts you're weighing and send them over this
> week? I want to know where it's wrong."

A yes = design partner forming. That's the win.

## Objections — pre-loaded for Charlie / Louder

- **"We've got agency relationships and our own sense of fees."** → "Totally — and post-Live-
  Nation you've got more data than most. This isn't to replace that; it's the one read in the
  room that isn't the agent's. For the booking at the edge of budget where you're guessing by
  ±£10k, a neutral second opinion is the value — not for the names you know cold."
- **"The fees are just estimates."** → "Correct, and I'll say that to your face — the fee *band*
  is curated. The *demand* side isn't: RA, Beatport, 1001Tracklists, streaming, routing, and the
  method's public. The gap is the signal. Honestly, the fastest way to make it sharper is for a
  buyer like you to tell me a couple of real numbers — then it's calibrated, not guessed."
- **"I already know who's hot."** → "You do. This isn't for the obvious headliners — it's for
  the one on the edge of the budget where it's a coin-flip on price. Which booking this season
  felt like that?"
- **"Who's behind this — an agency?"** → "Nobody. No act, no agency, no kickback. That's the
  whole point — it's the one number in the negotiation that isn't selling you something."
  *(This is the moat. Lead with it the moment trust wobbles.)*

## After the call (capture, don't forget)

- **Log every real fee he reveals** into `backend/fee_anchors.json` (real quoted/contracted
  numbers only — never a guess). Each one is a verified anchor; goal is 30–40. This call is your
  first source of them.
- Note his **Q3 unit and number** — that's a pricing data point, not a one-off.
- If he said yes to "run the next 3": do it within 48h while the call is warm. That's the
  Pitch-Link loop starting.
- Drop his objections + the £ he'd pay into `GTM.md` — it's your real pricing page being written.

---

## One-line reminders

- It's a **feedback call**. Curiosity > persuasion. Ask, then shut up, then write the exact words.
- The **neutrality** line is your strongest card. Play it when trust is the blocker.
- **Don't defend the price. Collect his.**
- The win is **"yes, run my next 3"** — not a signup.
