# PEAKTIME — customer discovery script

The one bet from the premortem: before adding another signal, find out whether a
buyer flinches with recognition. This is a **problem-discovery** guide, not a demo.
The goal is to learn whether the pain you built for is real and who feels it most —
*not* to pitch. If you're talking more than ~30% of the time, you're selling, not
learning.

**Target: 10 conversations** — 5 bookers/promoters, 5 artist managers. 20–25 min each.
Two tracks below because the two segments have opposite relationships to the data
(buyers want to pay less; managers want to prove worth more).

**The Mom Test rule:** ask about their life and past behaviour, never about your
idea or the future. "Would you use a tool that…" gets you lies. "Walk me through
the last time you…" gets you truth. Never mention PEAKTIME until the last question.

---

## Track A — Bookers / promoters / festival buyers

**Screen:** has personally booked or priced talent in the last 6 months.

1. **Walk me through the last DJ you booked who wasn't a sure thing.** How did you
   decide they were worth it, and how did you land on the fee?
   *(Listening for: gut vs. data, what sources they already trust, where it felt risky.)*

2. **Tell me about a booking that disappointed — paid too much, or the room didn't
   fill.** What would have tipped you off beforehand?
   *(Listening for: the cost of the pain, and whether any signal could have caught it.)*

3. **When you're sizing up an act you don't know well, what do you actually open?**
   Walk me through the tabs. *(Listening for: the real workflow — Spotify, RA, IG,
   asking a friend. This is your competition; it's usually "a mate's opinion.")*

4. **Last time you and an agent disagreed on a fee — what happened?** What would
   have changed the conversation?
   *(Listening for: whether an external number has leverage, or just offends.)*

5. **How do you decide who's worth booking *before* they're obvious / expensive?**
   Has betting early ever paid off or burned you?
   *(Listening for: appetite for the momentum/early-signal angle — your core claim.)*

**Only now:** "I've been building a demand index that scores and prices these acts.
Can I show you for two minutes and you tell me where it's wrong?" — then shut up
and watch where they lean in vs. glaze over.

---

## Track B — Artists / managers

**Screen:** manages or self-manages an act that gets booked (any tier).

1. **Last time you negotiated a fee or a slot — how did you argue your act's worth?**
   What did you bring to the table? *(Listening for: do they need ammunition, and
   what form does credible ammunition take?)*

2. **Tell me about a time you felt your act was underrated or mispriced by the
   industry.** What would have helped you prove it?
   *(Listening for: the "where do I really stand" pain — possibly your warmer wedge.)*

3. **How do you currently track whether your act is rising or stalling?** What do
   you check, how often? *(Listening for: existing tools, blind spots, frequency.)*

4. **Has a number or chart ever changed a booking conversation in your favour —
   or against you?** *(Listening for: whether third-party data has real currency.)*

5. **If an outside index ranked your act publicly, what's your gut reaction?**
   *(Listening for: the reputational landmine — do they see upside or threat?
   This tells you if public ranking helps or repels your own supply side.)*

**Only now:** show them their own act's page. Watch for "that's wrong" (engagement)
vs. "huh, neat" (indifference) vs. "take that down" (the #7 risk, live).

## What you're actually testing

| Hypothesis | Killed if you hear… | Confirmed if you hear… |
|---|---|---|
| Booking runs on gut and that hurts | "Our process is dialled, no real misses" | unprompted stories of overpaying / empty rooms |
| An external number has leverage | "An algorithm's number is meaningless to an agent" | "I'd screenshot that into the negotiation" |
| Early/momentum signal is the value | "I only care once they're proven" | "betting early is the whole game" |
| They'd pay £49–499/mo | shrug, "maybe", "if it were free" | "what's it cost / how do I get it" *unprompted* |
| Public ranking attracts, not repels | "don't ever rank me publicly" | "where am I — can I share this?" |

## Calling it

After 10: you're looking for a **repeated, specific, expensive** pain that the same
segment describes unprompted — and a pay signal that arrives *before* you mention
price. If 7+ bookers describe overpaying but recoil at an external fee number, the
wedge is **artists/managers proving worth**, not buyers cutting cost — pivot the
positioning, not the product. If neither segment has the pain, that's the most
valuable result this exercise can give you: stop building, and you found out for the
cost of ten coffees instead of another year of commits.
