# PEAKTIME — Sharpest Value Prop: Cross-Segment Synthesis

**Round:** Synthetic pilot (Cookiy), N=4 per segment, US+UK English, 15-min reaction-task interviews.
**Status:** Hypothesis-generating. Synthetic personas reasoned *about* PEAKTIME from briefed context — they did not truly browse thedjrankings.com, and the "check Value Gap vs. a real recent quote" deep cut was not genuinely testable. Treat as directional; confirm with the real-participant round.

Per-segment reports: `report-bookers.md`, `report-artists.md`, `report-fans.md`.

---

## The one-line verdict per segment

| Segment | Sharpest value prop | What's noise to them |
|---|---|---|
| **Bookers / Promoters** | **Value Gap** — as a negotiation lever against inflated agent quotes (a "buy signal") | **Momentum** — distrusted as gameable viral hype that doesn't convert to club tickets |
| **Artists / Management** | **Value Gap + Momentum** — as B2B proof to justify fee increases to buyers ("objective, not bragging") | Public/vanity framing — they want leverage, not a leaderboard |
| **Fans** | **Momentum** — a top-of-funnel *discovery trigger* (who's breaking, before friends catch on) | **Value Gap** — irrelevant + distrusted; it's a B2B metric |

---

## Five cross-segment throughlines

**1. Value Gap is the monetizable wedge.** Both paying audiences (bookers *and* artists/managers) independently latched onto it as the thing worth money — because it does a job they can't do today: prove an artist is under/over-priced with an objective third party. This is the B2B money signal.

**2. Momentum is the engagement wedge, not the money wedge.** Fans love it as a discovery spark; pros distrust it as "easily gamed or exaggerated by agents." Same feature, opposite reception. Lead with Momentum for the *audience funnel*, lead with Value Gap for the *paid product*.

**3. Transparency is the universal trust unlock — and your existing moat.** "Black box" was a stated *dealbreaker* for managers and a skepticism trigger for fans. Your published "How It Works" page is the asset; the gap is that it lives on a separate page. Surface per-metric "how this is calculated" tooltips directly on each signal.

**4. The credibility gap is local/live context.** Every booker said global digital metrics are noise until they correlate with **local ticket velocity and tour routing** ("if the room's half empty, that's real"). This is the single biggest risk to the Value Gap: if your fee estimate isn't anchored to venue size / regional comps, pros won't override their spreadsheets.

**5. Distribution: fans want push, not a destination.** Fans explicitly don't want another website to check — they want a skimmable weekly email / group-chat-shareable card. Social currency ("first to spot the next big thing") is the growth engine, not site visits.

## What this implies for the build & monetization

**Maps cleanly onto the dual-brand model:**
- **PEAKTIME (social + email) = the fan funnel.** A weekly "Movers / Breakout" Scout Report email, built for forwarding. Free. Drives SEO, brand, and top-of-funnel. Don't monetize fans directly.
- **thedjrankings.com Pro = the B2B product.** Value Gap is the hero feature.

**Pro tier (Bookers/Promoters) — Value Gap, productized:**
- Anchor Value Gap in **local/venue-size comps and routing**, not an abstract global number. *(This is the make-or-break.)*
- Package it as a **negotiation playbook**: the exact comps/data to counter an inflated agent quote.
- Add a **"Club vs. Viral" filter** on Momentum so it stops reading as hype.
- Pricing confirmed directionally: **~£75/mo solo, ~£300/mo team.** Charge for *leverage*, not raw data.

**A distinct Artist/Manager surface (possible separate tier):**
- **"Private Pitch Link"** — a time-limited, read-only Momentum + Value Gap view a manager sends to a buyer. Serves their exact "share with promoters, never on social" behavior.
- **"Gatekeeper mode"** — managers want to *hide* rankings from their own artists (mental-health concern). Notable, unprompted, and a real wedge.

## Caveats / what the real round must confirm
- Synthetic, N=4/segment; bookers persona-gate failed (need 3+ per cluster). Small, directional only.
- The core Value Gap accuracy test ("does it match a real recent quote?") was **not actually exercised** — synthetic users had no real bookings or live site to check. This is the #1 thing the real round must validate.
- Whether the £75/£300 price points hold with real buyers who have real budgets and procurement.
