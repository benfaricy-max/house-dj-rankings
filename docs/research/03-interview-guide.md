# PEAKTIME — Interview Guide

## Research Context
* **The Big Q:** Which signal/feature is the sharpest, most-trusted value prop per segment?
* **Key Decisions:** What to lead with, what to gate, what to post.
* **The Little q:** "Tell me about the last time you needed to figure out whether an artist was *worth it* — worth booking, worth backing, or worth going to see. Start wherever feels natural."

## Interviewer Reminders
> - You're the apprentice; they're the master. There are no wrong answers.
> - After the opening prompt, wait a full 60 seconds. Let them fill the silence.
> - When they react to a screen, try "Oh?" before a longer probe. Mirror their words.
> - Never sell or defend the product. If they misread a chart, that *is* the finding — study it, don't correct it.
> - Separate "interesting" from "I'd act on this." Always push to the second.

## Session Outline

### 1. Introduction & Orientation (5 min)
"Thanks for your time. I'm researching how people in the scene judge which artists are rising or worth backing. You're here because of your experience with [booking / performing / following the scene]. I want to learn from you — no right or wrong answers, and I'm not testing you. I'd like to record so I can focus on the conversation. OK?" *(Get verbal consent.)*

### 2. Warm-up & Current Behavior (10 min)
- **Icebreaker:** "How do you describe what you do in the scene to someone outside it?"
- **The Little q** (above). Then excavate the *current* workflow:
  - "Walk me through what you actually did, step by step. What did you look at?"
  - "Who or what did you trust in that moment? Who did you *not* trust?"
  - "What did it cost you to get that wrong — or right?"
- *This maps the status-quo tool (gut, an agent's word, RA follower counts) the product has to beat. Don't mention PEAKTIME yet.*

### 3. Reaction Task — Stimulus Walkthrough (25 min)
*Share the live site (or 3–4 screenshots). Stay quiet. Let them click and narrate.*
- **Open prompt:** "I'll share a site. Just talk out loud as you look — what you think it is, what you'd click, what you'd ignore."
- **First-impression probes:** "What is this, in your words?" · "Who do you think it's for?" · "What's the first thing your eye went to?"
- **Then surface each candidate value prop only if they don't reach it themselves, and for each ask the same three questions:**

  | Signal to expose | The three probes (every time) |
  |---|---|
  | Overall ranking | "Do you believe this order? What would make you trust it more or less?" |
  | **Momentum** (who's accelerating) | "What would you do with this, if anything?" |
  | **Value Gap** (underpriced vs. demand — the buy signal) | "Has this changed a decision you'd make?" |
  | **Reach vs. Credibility** chart | "Would you pay for this — and what would it have to do to be worth it?" |
  | **Scene Score** + How-It-Works methodology | (trust probe) "Does seeing the formula change how much you believe it?" |

- **Segment-specific deep cuts:**
  - *Bookers:* "Pull up an artist you've booked or quoted recently. Does what you see here match what you know? Where's it wrong?" → the **Value Gap / fee benchmark** is the suspected wedge — pressure-test it hard.
  - *Artists/Mgmt:* "Find yourself or your artist. What's your gut reaction?" Watch for ego vs. utility. → probe whether they'd *share* a good ranking, dispute a bad one, or pay to influence/monitor it.
  - *Fans:* "Which of these would make you come back next week?" → test the **Movers / Breakout** as the habit hook, and whether they'd share it.

- *If they generalize ("I'd probably use that"), redirect: "Can you think of a specific recent booking/gig/night where this would've changed what you did?"*

### 4. Retrospective & Wrap-up (8 min)
- **Forced rank:** "Of everything you saw, which *one* thing was actually useful to you — and which was noise?"
- **Trust gut-check:** "What would have to be true for you to rely on this instead of [their current method]?"
- **Willingness-to-pay (bookers/artists):** "If this lived behind a subscription, what part — if any — is worth paying for? What's the wrong thing to charge for?"
- **Summary check:** "It sounds like the thing that mattered to you was [X], and [Y] didn't land. Did I get that right?"
- **Magic wand:** "Wave a wand — change one thing about this and it becomes a must-use for you. What is it?"
- **Referral:** "Who else in the scene should I talk to about this?"
- **Close:** thank them, confirm incentive.
