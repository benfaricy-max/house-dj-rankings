# PEAKTIME — Interview Guide (15-min variant, Cookiy)

Compressed from the 45–60 min guide to fit Cookiy's 15-minute interview cap.
Protects the forced-rank that answers the Big Q; trims warm-up and per-signal deep-probing.

## Shared structure (all three segments)

**(1) Context — ~2 min**
- "How do you currently decide whether an artist is *worth it* — worth booking, backing, or going to see? Who or what do you trust today?"

**(2) Reaction task on live site (thedjrankings.com) — ~9 min**
- First impressions: "What is this? Who's it for? What did your eye go to first?"
- React to each signal — for each, probe: *Do you believe it? What would you DO with it? Has it changed a decision?*
  - Overall ranking
  - **Momentum** (who's accelerating)
  - **Value Gap** (underpriced vs. demand — the buy signal)
  - **Reach vs. Credibility** (festival headliner vs. DJ's DJ)
  - **Scene Score** + published methodology
- Drive toward a forced ranking: most useful signal vs. noise.

**(3) Wrap — ~4 min**
- Forced rank: single most useful thing, biggest noise.
- "What would have to be true to rely on this over [current method]?"
- Willingness to pay (bookers/artists): what's worth paying for, what's the *wrong* thing to charge for.
- Magic-wand change.

**Moderator stance:** apprentice, not seller. Never defend the product. Push every "interesting" to "would you act on it?" with a specific recent example.

## Segment deep-cuts
- **Bookers/Promoters:** have them recall an artist they recently quoted/booked and check whether the **Value Gap / fee benchmark** matches reality. Suspected wedge.
- **Artists/Management:** have them find themselves/their artist; watch ego vs. utility; probe whether they'd share a good ranking, dispute a bad one, or pay to monitor/influence standing.
- **Fans:** which signal makes them come back next week; **Movers/Breakout** as the habit hook; would they share it. Lighter on willingness-to-pay.
