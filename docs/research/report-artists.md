# PEAKTIME — Signal Value Proposition Research (Artists & Management) Analysis Report

## Research Overview
- **Methodology:** Qualitative in-depth interviews with transcript-first synthesis.
- **Participant Count:** 4 participants (Artists & Management).
- **Research Question:** How do artists and managers evaluate and utilize PEAKTIME's data signals (Momentum, Value Gap, Reach vs. Credibility) for booking strategy and negotiation leverage?
- **Timeframe:** Recent (Synthesis based on provided transcript data).

## Key Findings

**1. "Momentum" and "Value Gap" signals are highly valued as B2B utilities for direct negotiation leverage, replacing subjective arguments.**
- **Statement:** Managers struggle to prove an artist's upward trajectory to buyers using subjective anecdotes. They view PEAKTIME's specific composite metrics—particularly the Value Gap and Momentum—as critical tools to justify fee increases and spot underpriced talent.
- **Evidence:** Participants explicitly noted past failures in negotiations due to a lack of objective momentum metrics. They want to use these signals to confidently push for higher guarantees.
- **Quotes:** 
  - *"If I’d had a solid index showing momentum or a ‘value gap’—meaning I was underpriced compared to demand—I could’ve pushed harder or justified the full $1,000. Without it, I just sounded like I was bragging, not negotiating."*
  - *"Last October, a club buyer told me the artist needed to prove they were “moving upward” before agreeing to raise fees... it felt subjective—no clear metric to show momentum. Made me wish for a better signal."*
  - *"I’d head straight to the Overall composite ranking to see where artists stand, then check the Momentum section—that’s the real gold for me. After that, maybe the Value Gap to spot who’s underpriced."*
- **Frequency:** High
- **Impact:** High
- **Confidence:** High
- **Participants:** P3, P4

**2. Users demand transparent, "anti-hype" metrics that distinguish real scene credibility from manufactured mainstream reach.**
- **Statement:** Participants are highly skeptical of digital vanity metrics, knowing they can be manipulated. They rely on PEAKTIME to cross-reference digital spikes with actual scene credibility to identify genuine demand versus "marketing noise."
- **Evidence:** Users actively look for discrepancies between streaming numbers and real-world bookings. A "black-box" algorithm is considered a dealbreaker because managers refuse to risk their reputation on subjective or easily manipulated data.
- **Quotes:** 
  - *"To me, Reach vs Credibility shows if an artist is mainstream buzz or underground respect. Like, big streaming numbers but no scene cred means hype without depth."*
  - *"If I saw obvious streaming spikes with no corresponding bookings or scene buzz, that’d ring alarm bells."*
  - *"I need to see how rankings or scores are built—what data sources, what weighting... If it’s unclear or too subjective, I don’t risk my artist’s rep showing it. That’s the dealbreaker."*
- **Frequency:** High
- **Impact:** High
- **Confidence:** High
- **Participants:** P3, P4, and general consensus.

**3. Real-world booking data and promoter feedback heavily outweigh digital vanity metrics.**
- **Statement:** While digital signals are useful, participants ultimately validate an artist's momentum through "on-the-ground" realities like sold-out rooms, gig frequency, and credible DJ endorsements.
- **Evidence:** Participants noted that promoters hold the ultimate truth regarding an artist's draw. Digital metrics that do not translate to ticket sales or fees are deprioritized.
- **Quotes:** 
  - *"Online spikes can be bought or boosted easily, but if the room’s half empty, that’s real. Plus, promoters hold the purse strings, so their honest feedback usually tells me if demand’s legit or just hype."*
  - *"I prioritize the signals that directly impact bookings—like gig frequency and fees, or credible DJ endorsements. Social media hype or press can be noisy, so if those don’t translate into actual shows or fees, they get lower priority."*
- **Frequency:** Low
- **Impact:** Medium
- **Confidence:** High
- **Participants:** P3, P4

**4. Managers act as protective gatekeepers, shielding artists from rankings to preserve mental health.**
- **Statement:** While data is viewed as a powerful B2B tool for managers and agents, it is seen as a potential hazard for artists. Managers actively avoid sharing rankings publicly or with their own artists to prevent ego clashes.
- **Evidence:** Participants explicitly stated they would use the tool selectively with promoters but keep it away from social media and the artists themselves.
- **Quotes:** 
  - *"If the ranking’s credible and transparent, I might share selectively with promoters or agents. But I’d avoid public social media as it could trigger unhealthy comparisons or ego clashes within the scene. It’s about leverage, not ego."*
- **Frequency:** Low
- **Impact:** Medium
- **Confidence:** High
- **Participants:** P4

## Participant Profiles

- **Participant 3 (P3): The Strategic Pragmatist**
  - **Segment:** Management / Strategy
  - **Key Perspective:** Views data not merely as a scorecard, but as a dynamic timing tool to dictate strategic leverage. They bridge digital metrics with on-the-ground live booking realities.
  - **Emotional Tone:** Analytical, pragmatic, and decisive.
  - **Notable Quote:** *"Right now, it feels static; I need dynamic insights to decide when to push or hold back."*
  - **Distinctive Viewpoint:** Demands that data be actionable in time—knowing exactly *when* to push for a higher guarantee based on converging digital and physical signals.

- **Participant 4 (P4): The Protective Gatekeeper**
  - **Segment:** Management / Representation
  - **Key Perspective:** Strictly separates B2B leverage from public ego. Rejects vanity metrics and black-box algorithms to safeguard both their artists' mental health and their own industry reputation.
  - **Emotional Tone:** Pragmatic, analytical, cautious, and highly protective.
  - **Notable Quote:** *"If it’s unclear or too subjective, I don’t risk my artist’s rep showing it. That’s the dealbreaker."*
  - **Distinctive Viewpoint:** Views PEAKTIME strictly as a B2B utility. They are highly protective of their artists' mental health, actively choosing to hide rankings from the artists to prevent "unhealthy comparisons."

## Contradictions & Tensions

- **Digital Hype vs. Physical Reality:** There is a strong tension between digital streaming numbers (which participants believe can be "bought or boosted easily") and physical room attendance ("if the room's half empty, that's real"). Participants do not trust digital metrics unless they are anchored by physical booking data.
- **B2B Utility vs. Public Ego:** Participants recognize the immense value of ranking and momentum data for negotiations, but strongly oppose making this data public or even sharing it with their own artists. The tension lies in needing the data to be authoritative enough for buyers, but hidden enough to prevent scene drama and mental health issues.

## Opportunity Areas

- **Methodology Transparency:** Because "black-box" metrics are a dealbreaker, there is a massive opportunity to build trust by exposing the exact weights and data sources behind the "Momentum" and "Value Gap" scores.
- **B2B-Specific Export/Sharing:** Since managers want to share data selectively with promoters but keep it off social media, creating a secure, professional "Pitch Deck" or "Negotiation View" export would directly serve their workflow.
- **Dynamic Timing Alerts:** Moving the platform from a static dashboard to a dynamic alert system (e.g., notifying a manager when an artist's momentum spikes) would fulfill the need for strategic timing insights.

## Recommendations

- **Design a "Methodology Tooltip" System:** For every composite score (Momentum, Value Gap, Reach vs. Credibility), implement a clear, accessible breakdown of the exact inputs (e.g., "This score is weighted by 40% gig frequency, 30% DJ co-signs, 30% streaming growth"). This directly addresses P4's dealbreaker regarding subjective, black-box data.
- **Develop a "Gatekeeper Mode" for Management Accounts:** Build a feature that allows managers to lock or hide specific vanity rankings from their artists' view (if artists have sub-accounts), while retaining full access to the B2B negotiation metrics (Value Gap) to protect artist mental health.
- **Create a "Private Pitch Link" Feature:** Allow managers to generate a time-sensitive, read-only link of an artist's "Momentum" and "Value Gap" data to send directly to club buyers and promoters. This satisfies the need for objective negotiation leverage while preventing public social media sharing.
- **Integrate Live-Data Proxies into the Momentum Algorithm:** Ensure the Momentum algorithm heavily weights (or explicitly correlates with) live performance proxies like gig frequency, venue size, or DJ endorsements, as users explicitly distrust pure streaming data.
