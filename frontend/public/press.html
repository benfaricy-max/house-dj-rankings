The demand index for electronic music

A neutral booking-demand index for house & techno DJs. Data, not hype.

PEAKTIME (thedjrankings.com) ranks 340+ house and techno DJs by booking demand rather than streaming popularity, and turns that into a neutral fee benchmark both a promoter and an agent can cite in the same negotiation. No agency, no label, no act behind it. The method is public.

Story angles

The most-streamed DJs aren't the most bookable

Streaming reach and booking demand barely correlate. Sven Väth pulls 23rd in booking demand on 104K monthly listeners; some acts with 10M+ listeners sit past 300th. PEAKTIME is the first to quantify the gap across the whole field.

Which DJs are underpriced

A neutral fee benchmark reads booking demand against known fee bands. Jayda G currently reads about 3x under her live demand. Fee bands are model-implied estimates; the demand signal is the measured data.

How it works

The index blends live booking signals (Resident Advisor venue tier and attendance, touring density), scene credibility, Beatport chart standing, what DJs actually play (1001Tracklists), and streaming, then weights credibility over raw reach. Full method: thedjrankings.com.
