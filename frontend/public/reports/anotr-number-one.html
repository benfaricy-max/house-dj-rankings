ANOTR is #1. The streaming charts don't show it yet.

Ben Faricy · June 2026

FISHER has 19.6M Spotify monthly listeners. ANOTR has 15.6M. FISHER ranks #11. ANOTR ranks #1. If that looks backwards, you're reading from the wrong data.

Spotify tells you who people listen to on the commute. It doesn't tell you who sells out fabric on a Tuesday, or which artists promoters are calling at 11pm trying to lock in a date before someone else does. Those are different signals. Until now, nobody was measuring them.

PEAKTIME ranks house DJs by booking demand: live event volume, venue tier, booking frequency, ticket sales. Not streams. Not fan votes. When the numbers ran, ANOTR came out at number one. They have a Beatport score of 99, a scene credibility score of 83, and are currently one of the most booked acts in the European club circuit. The streaming numbers are catching up to what the scene already knows.

Spotify tells you who people listen to at work. PEAKTIME tells you who promoters are fighting over. Those are different lists.

FISHER is a useful illustration. He sits at number eleven on our rankings, which reflects a real and legitimate profile: global touring, festival headlining, an Ibiza residency. The ranking isn't dismissing him. It's placing him accurately against a field where ANOTR's booking density, venue calibre, and scene standing currently run higher. Two different artists, both correctly placed.

The DJ Mag Top 100 is fan-voted. It captures passion. This index captures something else: who promoters are actually booking, at what venues, and how often. Those two pictures don't always point at the same artists. Where they diverge is where the interesting decisions get made.

"I know there are platforms like Viberate and Beatstats, but your platform feels much better."
Charlie Holden, lead booker, Louder UK — who said he would pay for a subscription today

Bookers don't need more streaming data. They already have it, and so does every competitor. What the existing tools don't give you is a read on who's actually moving in the market right now, before the charts catch up. Underground scenes shift faster than algorithms. An artist can go from credible to essential in six months without their Spotify numbers moving at all.
