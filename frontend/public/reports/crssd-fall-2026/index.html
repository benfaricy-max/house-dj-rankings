Key wins & watch-outs

Smartest buy

Prospa — booked low, demand high

#4 on the index, momentum 56, Beatport 95, RA 88 — flagged a Strong Buy (demand implies a £70–150K tier vs an estimated ~$42K here). The fee almost certainly jumps before the next CRSSD.

Value mid-card

KETTAMA & Carlita punch up

KETTAMA carries a 66 live-conversion score — his RA crowd dwarfs his streaming, so he overdelivers on a festival stage relative to a ~$48K fee. Carlita flags underpriced too. Classic margin bookings.

Paying for the name

The headline b2b is a marquee, not a deal

Chris Lake b2b Disclosure eats 16% of the budget, and both read Priced ahead — fees hotter than current momentum (Disclosure 20, Chris Lake 5, both flat). It sells the festival; it isn't a value play.

Breakout to watch

Rossi. is accelerating fast

Momentum 75 — among the steepest on the whole bill — at an estimated b2b-shared fee. Booked early enough that the b2b-with-Carlita slot looks like a steal in hindsight if the climb continues.

Market saturation — this is a San Diego date

West-coast fatigue risk
  • Chris Lake is heavily worked on the US West Coast — Las Vegas (2 shows/3mo) and SF/Oakland — so the "event" scarcity is low for a SoCal crowd.
  • KETTAMA is maxed in SF/Oakland (5 shows/3mo, saturation 100) and has already hit San Diego this quarter — some local-fatigue risk despite the value.
  • Mathame recently played both San Diego and LA — regionally present.
  • Upside: Disclosure, Prospa, Carlita, I Hate Models, Ben UFO, Helena Hauff carry their saturation in other markets — comparatively fresh for Southern California.

Who actually drives ticket sales

Three different jobs on this bill: the marquee (Chris Lake b2b Disclosure, Skepta, Mochakk, Sonny Fodera) sells the on-sale; the scene-credibility techno block (I Hate Models, Ben UFO, Helena Hauff, 999999999, VTSS — all high RA booking demand) sells the festival's underground bona fides and converts the hardcore; and the value mid-card (Prospa, KETTAMA, Carlita, Jamback) over-delivers relative to spend. The hidden engine is live-conversion: acts like KETTAMA and Helena Hauff turn modest streaming into outsized room demand — exactly what a discerning buyer wants.
